## Step 1: Unsubscribe

### Platform Search Strategies

**In Gmail:**
1. Click the search bar at the top
2. Type: `unsubscribe`
3. Press Enter to see all emails with unsubscribe links
4. Use bulk selection (click checkbox, then "Select all conversations that match this search")

**In Apple Mail:**
1. Press Cmd+F (Mac) to open search
2. Type: `unsubscribe`
3. Review results and delete in batches

**In Outlook:**
1. Click the search box at the top
2. Type: `unsubscribe`
3. Use Ctrl+A to select all, then delete unwanted emails

### The Decision Framework

For each email list, ask these 3 questions:

**Revenue Test:** Has this helped me make money in the last 3 months?

**Action Test:** Do I actually read and act on these emails when they arrive?

**Value Test:** Would I pay $10/month for this content?

If you answer "no" to all three, **unsubscribe immediately**.

### Common Unsubscribes

- Marketing blogs you bookmarked but never read
- Daily deal sites and coupon emails (unless you actually use them)
- Software companies you trialed but didn't purchase
- Industry newsletters that don't apply to your specific niche
- Social media notifications (turn these off in the apps instead)
- E-commerce stores you bought from once years ago
- Conference and event promotions for events you won't attend

## Troubleshooting Common Issues

### "My Filters Aren't Working"

**Gmail Issues:**
- Check if emails are going to spam first (check spam folder)
- Filters are case-sensitiveâ€”use OR operators: `Company OR company`
- Some emails might need body text filters, not just "From" filters

**Apple Mail Issues:**
- Rules only apply to new incoming emails, not existing ones
- Check rule orderâ€”rules are processed top to bottom
- Make sure your rule criteria aren't too broad or narrow

**Outlook Issues:**
- Rules might be disabledâ€”check Rules & Alerts settings
- Server-side rules have limitsâ€”keep them simple
- Check if rules are running on all folders or just inbox

### "I'm Still Getting Too Many Emails"

- Audit your subscriptions monthlyâ€”new ones sneak in constantly
- Use email aliasesâ€”create separate addresses for different purposes
- Be selective with business cardsâ€”don't give your main email to everyone
- Use contact forms instead of exposing your email publicly

### "Important Emails Are Getting Buried"

- Create a VIP/Priority list in your email client
- Use separate email addresses for different business functions
- Train sendersâ€”ask key clients to use specific subject line formats
- Set up phone backupâ€”give clients your number for true emergencies
