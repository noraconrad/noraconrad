## Step 1: The Two-Touch Email System

### Morning Email Session (15 minutes)

**Timing:** First 15 minutes of your designated work time  
**Goal:** Handle urgent items and set communication priorities for the day

**The 4-Step Morning Process:**

**Step 1: Emergency Scan (2 minutes)**

- Check `ðŸ”¥ CLIENTS - ACTION NEEDED` folder only
- Look for true emergencies: system failures, moved deadlines, payment issues
- Ignore everything else during this scan

**Step 2: Quick Response Batch (8 minutes)**

- Use templates for responses that take <2 minutes
- Send "received, will respond by [time]" for items needing longer responses
- Forward delegatable items immediately
- Schedule complex responses for specific afternoon time slots

**Step 3: Daily Priority Setting (3 minutes)**

- Identify 2-3 emails requiring detailed responses today
- Block specific times in your calendar for these responses
- Move non-urgent items to tomorrow's list

**Step 4: Inbox Zero (2 minutes)**

- Archive everything that's been processed
- Leave only items with scheduled response times
- Close email completely until afternoon session

**Morning Session Rules:**

âŒ No checking newsletters, promotions, or "read later" folders  
âŒ No starting lengthy email conversations  
âŒ No processing emails that can wait until afternoon  

âœ… Focus only on same-day client needs

âœ… Set clear expectations for longer responses

### Afternoon Email Session (15 minutes)

**Timing:** 4 PM or 2 hours before your designated work-stop time  
**Goal:** Complete the day's email commitments and prepare for tomorrow

**The 3-Step Afternoon Process:**

**Step 1: Commitment Completion (10 minutes)**

- Complete responses you scheduled from the morning session
- Send any emails scheduled throughout the day
- Follow up on urgent items from the morning

**Step 2: New Email Processing (4 minutes)**

- Quick scan of new arrivals since morning
- Template responses for simple items
- Schedule complex new items for tomorrow morning if not urgent

**Step 3: Tomorrow Preparation (1 minute)**

- Review tomorrow's calendar for email context
- Set up any templates or information needed for known tomorrow emails
- Clear inbox to zero

**Afternoon Session Rules:**

### The "Urgent vs. Important" Client Education

**True Emergencies (Call immediately):**

- Website/system completely down affecting business operations
- Project deadline moved up with <24 hours notice
- Payment processing issues

## Step 3: Technology & Environment Setup

### Creating Email-Free Zones

**Physical Spaces:**

- **Dining room/kitchen:** No phones during family meals
- **Bedroom:** Email apps logged out after 8 PM
- **Car:** Phone in "Do Not Disturb" during family activities
- **Living room:** Designated "device-free" evening hours

**Digital Boundaries:**

**WORK HOURS (9 AM - 6 PM):**

- Email notifications: ON for VIP contacts only
- Phone calls: All contacts
- Text messages: All contacts

**FAMILY HOURS (6 PM - 9 AM):**

- Email notifications: OFF completely
- Phone calls: Family and emergency contacts only
- Text messages: Family and close friends only

**WEEKEND MODE:**

- Email notifications: OFF
- Work apps: Logged out or hidden
- Emergency contact: Available via phone

### The Work-Stop Ritual

Create a physical transition between work and family time:

**5-Minute Work-Stop Routine:**

1. Complete final email check (afternoon session)
2. Close all work applications on computer and phone
3. Put devices in designated "off-limits" location
4. Change clothes (work attire â†’ family/relaxation clothes)
5. Do something physical (walk, stretch, deep breathing)
6. Say out loud: "Work is finished for today"

**Why this works:**

- Creates clear mental separation between work and family time
- Reduces the temptation to "quickly check email"
- Signals to family that you're transitionally present
- Helps your brain shift from work mode to family mode

## Step 4: Managing Email Anxiety

### The "5-Minute Rule" for Email Urges

When you feel compelled to check email outside scheduled times:

1. **Acknowledge the urge:** "I want to check email right now"
2. **Ask the reality check question:** "Will someone be in actual danger or will I lose significant money if I don't check this for 2 hours?"
3. **If the answer is no:** Set a 5-minute timer and do something else
4. **Alternative activity:** Talk to family member, take a walk, drink water, do quick exercise
5. Usually the urge passes before the timer goes off

### "Emergency" Reality Check

**Questions to ask yourself:**

- In your entire business history, how many true email emergencies have you had?
- What's the worst thing that happened when you didn't check email for 4 hours?
- Do your most important clients have your phone number for real emergencies?
- Are you checking email to solve problems or to satisfy anxiety?

**Common "Emergencies" That Aren't:**

- Client questions that can wait until tomorrow
- Administrative requests from vendors
- Newsletter content or promotional emails
- Social media notifications
- Non-urgent project updates

## Step 6: Weekly & Monthly Maintenance

### Friday Email Wrap-Up (15 minutes)

**End-of-Week Process:**

**Complete postponed items (8 minutes)**
- Follow-ups scheduled for Friday
- Administrative emails that accumulated
- Items moved to "end of week" during busy days

**System maintenance (4 minutes)**
- Empty "READ LATER" folder completely (delete 90%, save 1-2 valuable items)
- Review filter effectiveness â€” emails going to right folders?
- Unsubscribe from new lists that appeared this week

**Weekend preparation (3 minutes)**
- Set up Monday morning context (review calendar for email needs)
- Clear inbox to zero for weekend break
- Set up out-of-office message if taking full weekend off

### Monthly Assessment (20 minutes)

**First Friday of Each Month:**

**Boundary Health Check:**

- How many times did I check email outside scheduled hours this month?
- What situations triggered boundary breaks?
- How has my family responded to increased presence?
- What felt sustainable vs. stressful about my routine?

**Client Relationship Review:**

- Any complaints about communication response times?
- Opportunities to improve template effectiveness?
- Clients who consistently respect vs. push boundaries?
- Adjustments needed for seasonal business changes?

**System Optimization:**

- Which email types still take too long to process?
- New automation opportunities based on repeated emails?
- Technology changes that could improve efficiency?
- Delegation opportunities for email management?
